The @OverheidEdits bot is probably my most successful and popular Twitter bot. It's been featured in the Dutch newspaper [de Volkskrant](https://www.volkskrant.nl/nieuws-achtergrond/wikipedia-pagina-nieuwsuur-aangepast-vanaf-ministerie-buitenlandse-zaken-nieuwsuur-spreek-je-uit-als-nieuw-zuur~be99ea77/), and on a few other media related outlets like [Villiamedia](https://www.villamedia.nl/artikel/ministerie-buza-bewerkte-wikipedia-pagina-nieuwsuur) and [ComputerIdee](https://www.computeridee.nl/nieuws/harry-potter-en-voetbal-dit-wijzigt-de-overheid-allemaal-op-wikipedia/).

The working principle of the bot is pretty simple. It monitors all the changes made to the Dutch Wikipedia website. And if an edit is made by from an IP address of a Dutch government organisation, it will tweet it. If users are logged into Wikipedia, their IP addresses are not publicly available so then it's not possible to show if a change was made by a Dutch government organisation.

I'm still astonished by the popularity of this bot. It currently has more than 4000 followers and more popular tweets easily get more than a 100.000 views. Which is a lot more than my personal account has ever seen.
